Methodology: For each county, compute early-vote liberal % in the 2025 race (Crawford-Schimel, mail + in-person absentee combined) and early-vote liberal % in the 2026 race (Taylor-Lazar). The difference is that county's early swing. Apply that swing to the same county's 2025 election day liberal % to get a projected election day liberal %. Estimate remaining election day vote per county by multiplying NYT's total_expected_vote by the county's 2025 election day share. Final Lib and Con per county = currently counted 2026 vote (early modes, fully in) + projected election day. Counties with no 2026 early data yet fall back to the statewide early swing. Wisconsin Supreme Court is officially nonpartisan; "Lib" and "Con" map to the Democratic-aligned and Republican-aligned candidates respectively, using nyt_id-based candidate identification (Crawford, Taylor → Lib; Schimel, Lazar → Con).
